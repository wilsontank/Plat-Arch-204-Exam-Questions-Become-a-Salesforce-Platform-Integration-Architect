<h2><strong>Detailed Breakdown of the Plat-Arch-204 Exam Domains and Weighting</strong></h2>

<p>The Salesforce Certified Platform Integration Architect exam evaluates your skills across six major domains. Understanding the weight of each section can help you plan your study strategy more effectively.</p>

<p><strong>1. Evaluate the Current System Landscape (8%)</strong></p>

<p>This section focuses on analyzing existing systems, data sources, and integration constraints. You must understand system dependencies, data ownership, latency considerations, and security requirements.</p>

<p><strong>2. Evaluate Business Needs (11%)</strong></p>

<p>Candidates are tested on their ability to gather and assess business requirements. This includes understanding functional needs, non-functional requirements, and organizational priorities that influence integration design.</p>

<p><strong>3. Translate Needs to Integration Requirements (22%)</strong></p>

<p>A critical section of the exam, this domain evaluates how well you can convert business needs into clear technical integration requirements. Topics include data synchronization, error handling, transaction boundaries, and performance expectations.</p>

<p><strong>4. Design Integration Solutions (28%)</strong></p>

<p>This is the most heavily weighted section. You must demonstrate expertise in selecting appropriate integration patterns such as request and reply, batch data synchronization, event-driven integrations, and platform events and Change Data Capture. You&#39;ll also need to consider scalability, fault tolerance, and security best practices.</p>

<p><strong>5. Build Solution (23%)</strong></p>

<p>Here, the exam tests your understanding of implementing integration designs using Salesforce tools and technologies. This includes APIs, middleware platforms, authentication mechanisms, and deployment considerations.</p>

<p><strong>6. Maintain Integration (8%)</strong></p>

<p>This section focuses on post-deployment activities such as monitoring, logging, error recovery, versioning, and ongoing optimization of integration solutions.</p>
